The 7 Approaches To Brand Management

From the book "Brand Management: Research, Theory and Practice" by Tilde Heding
Books, Brand Management, Strategy

"Brand Management: Research, Theory and Practice" by Tilde Heding, Mogens Bjerre, and Charlotte Knudtzen offers a comprehensive look at seven distinct brand approaches. Each approach provides unique insights and strategies for managing brands effectively. Let's dive into these approaches and explore scenarios where each is suitable for brands in practice.

1. The Economic Approach
This approach views the brand as a financial asset and focuses on maximizing brand value through strategic investments and marketing activities. It emphasizes the importance of brand equity and return on investment (ROI).
Scenario: A multinational corporation looking to enhance its brand value and attract investors would benefit from the economic approach. By focusing on financial metrics and ROI, the company can make data-driven decisions to maximize brand equity.

2. The Identity Approach
The identity approach emphasizes the importance of a strong and consistent brand identity. It focuses on creating a unique brand image that resonates with consumers and differentiates the brand from competitors.
Scenario: A luxury fashion brand aiming to establish a distinct and recognizable identity would benefit from the identity approach. By developing a cohesive brand image and consistently communicating it across all touchpoints, the brand can build a strong and loyal customer base.

3. The Consumer-Based Approach
This approach centres on understanding consumer perceptions and experiences with the brand. It emphasizes the importance of creating positive brand associations and delivering exceptional value to consumers.
Scenario: A consumer electronics company launching a new product would benefit from the consumer-based approach. By conducting market research and understanding consumer preferences, the company can tailor its marketing strategies to create positive brand associations and drive product adoption.

4. The Personality Approach
The personality approach views the brand as having human-like characteristics and focuses on creating a relatable and engaging brand personality. It emphasizes the importance of emotional connections with consumers.
Scenario: A beverage brand targeting millennials would benefit from the personality approach. By developing a fun and relatable brand personality, the brand can connect with its target audience on an emotional level and build brand loyalty.

5. The Relational Approach
This approach focuses on building strong relationships between the brand and its consumers. It emphasizes the importance of trust, loyalty, and long-term engagement.
Scenario: A subscription-based service, such as a streaming platform, would benefit from the relational approach. By fostering strong relationships with subscribers through personalized experiences and excellent customer service, the brand can increase retention and reduce churn.

6. The Community Approach
The community approach emphasizes the importance of creating and nurturing brand communities. It focuses on fostering a sense of belonging and shared identity among consumers.
Scenario: A fitness brand promoting a healthy lifestyle would benefit from the community approach. By creating a supportive and engaged community of fitness enthusiasts, the brand can build a loyal customer base and encourage word-of-mouth marketing.

7. The Cultural Approach
This approach views the brand as a cultural artifact and focuses on its role in shaping and reflecting cultural values and norms. It emphasizes the importance of cultural relevance and authenticity.
Scenario: A sustainable fashion brand aiming to promote eco-friendly practices would benefit from the cultural approach. By aligning the brand with cultural values of sustainability and environmental consciousness, the brand can attract socially conscious consumers and build a strong reputation.

By understanding and applying these seven brand approaches, brand managers can develop effective strategies that resonate with consumers and create lasting value. Each approach offers unique insights and tools for managing brands in different contexts, making "Brand Management: Research, Theory and Practice" an invaluable resource for anyone involved in brand management.
